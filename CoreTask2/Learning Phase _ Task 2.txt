Subtask 2
Generative Modeling on CIFAR-10
Overview
In this assignment, you will explore generative computer vision models by fine-tuning and analyzing modern image generation architectures.
You will be divided into groups, with each group assigned one of the following model families:
* Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)
* Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)
* Diffusion Models
Dataset
* GAN and Diffusion Groups: CIFAR-10 (10 object categories including airplane, automobile, bird, etc.). You may train on the full dataset, selected classes, or custom subsets.
* VAE Groups: To properly explore latent space manipulation (image editing/morphing), you will use a face or attribute-based dataset instead of CIFAR-10. Suggested datasets include dSprites, a subset of CelebA (downscaled to 64x64), or the Anime Faces dataset.

Model Initialization Requirement
For this assignment, you are not required to train a generative model entirely from scratch.
Instead, each group should begin with a publicly available pretrained implementation corresponding to their assigned model family.
Your task is to:
1. Obtain pretrained weights from a reputable source.
2. Re-implement or wrap the model architecture within your own PyTorch codebase.
3. Load the pretrained weights into your implementation.
4. Fine-tune the model on the assigned dataset.
5. Conduct experiments and analysis on the resulting model.
Suggested Workflow
Data Exploration
Analyze:
* class distribution,
* image characteristics,
* intra-class variation,
* preprocessing choices.
Visualize samples and understand the dataset before training.
Baseline Generation
Obtain a working pretrained baseline model for your assigned paradigm.
Examples:
* Convolutional VAE
* DCGAN
* DDPM
Train and generate initial samples.
Model Improvements
Possible directions include:
VAE Groups
* latent dimension studies,
* β-VAE,
* deeper encoders,
* different reconstruction losses,
* Latent Space Interpolation.
GAN Groups
* DCGAN variants,
* WGAN,
* spectral normalization,
* discriminator architecture changes.
Diffusion Groups
* different noise schedules,
* timestep sampling,
* U-Net variations,
* Inference speed improvements.
Analysis
Investigate:
* image quality,
* diversity,
* failure cases,
* training stability,
* mode collapse (GANs),
* latent space behavior (VAEs),
* sampling quality (Diffusion).
Ablations and Experiments
